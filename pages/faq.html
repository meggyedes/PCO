Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about HZ University facilities, procedures, and student life. This collection helps me and fellow students navigate university life more effectively.

HZ offers two printing options to suit your needs:

Web Print: Print (but not plot) from all devices
Mobility Print: Print and plot from all devices (plotting only available on Windows or Apple laptops)
Printing Documents (Web Print)
  1. Open https://hz.mynetpay.nl/ in your browser
  2. Log in with your HZ username and password
  3. Tap Web Print
  4. At Step 1: Select a printer, check the settings (format, single/double sided, etc.)
  5. Click in the field at Step 2: Select files to choose your files (you can also drag and drop)
  6. Click on Upload files now
  7. Use your HZ card at any printer to collect your printed documents
Plotting and Printing Documents (Mobility Print)
2.1 Installing Mobility Print
  1. Open https://hz.mynetpay.nl/content/printdeploy/driverprint.html in your browser
  2. Tap the desired operating system (the file will now be downloaded)
  3. Run the downloaded file and follow the instructions
  4. Tap Sign in to install
  5. Enter your HZ username and password and click Sign in to install
  6. The HZ printer and plotter are now installed and selectable in different software
2.2 Printing
  1. In the desired application, choose print and select HZ_Printer
  2. Log on to the printer by scanning your HZ card or entering your username/password
  3. On the printer, press Print Release
  4. After printing, log off by pressing the log-off symbol on the printer
2.3 Plotting
Note: First save your document (AutoCAD, Word, PowerPoint, etc.) in PDF format and open it in Adobe Acrobat Reader for best results.
  1. Open the document (preferably in Adobe Acrobat)
  2. Click on File > Print...
  3. Select HZ_Plotter [HZ-University](Mobility) and click Properties
  4. Go to the Paper/Quality tab and select the desired size
  5. Go to the Color tab and check Print in color if you want to plot in color
  6. Click OK
  7. Under Page Sizing & Handling, select Fit if the image may be enlarged to the size of the paper
  8. Click Print
  9. Log in to the printer using your HZ card or username/password
  10. Press Print Release on the printer
  11. After plotting, press the log-off symbol on the printer to sign out

Scanning documents at HZ is easy with the multifunction printers:

Important: Scanning to a private/external mailbox is not possible. Please do not change the scanner settings.
  1. Log on to the printer using your HZ card or by entering your HZ username & password
  2. Choose the Scan option in the menu
  3. Place the original(s) on the feeder or glass plate
  4. (Optional) For double-sided scanning:
    • Press Change settings
    • For duplex mode, select the 2-sided option
  5. Press Start
  6. After scanning, press the log-off symbol on the printer to log off

Booking project spaces and resources through the Self Service Portal:

  1. Go to the home page of the Self Service Portal
  2. Click on Reservations
  3. Choose the appropriate category and search for the "resource" you want to reserve
  4. If necessary, use the Type filter to turn certain items on/off in the overview
  5. Select the day when the resource is borrowed
  6. By dragging the left mouse button, indicate the time period when the resource is borrowed
  7. Click Next
  8. Read the loan conditions, check the reservation and click Next
  9. Fill in the necessary information and click Submit request
Booking Tips: Book in advance for popular time slots, and remember to cancel if you can't make it so others can use the space.

Official parking information for HZ University campuses:

Vlissingen Campus (Edisonweg)
Main Campus Parking: Around HZ and near the building on the Prins Hendrikweg (opposite the main entrance of the main HZ location on the Edisonweg) parking places are available.
  • General Parking: Ample parking space available in the HZ car park
  • Car Park Access: From Bergen op Zoom, follow the A58 until the very end. Turn left at the traffic lights, then immediately go right following the sign to the station. Cross the railway and follow the road (Oude Veerhavenweg). The entrance to the HZ car park is on the right.
  • HZ Tower Parking: For appointments in the HZ Tower, parking spaces are available directly under and next to the HZ Tower. If no space there, use the general HZ car park right next to the HZ Tower.
  • Accessibility: Special parking spaces are available for people with disabilities and/or chronic conditions
Important: There is a parking ban in the area between Edisonweg, Oude Veerhavenweg and Prins Hendrikweg. Only park your car in the designated parking spaces.
Middelburg Campus (Het Groene Woud)
  • On-Campus Parking: It is not possible to park directly at the HZ on Het Groene Woud
  • Accessibility Parking: There is 1 special parking space available on the grounds for people with disabilities and/or chronic conditions
  • Kousteensedijk Car Park: The car park on Kousteensedijk has a number of spaces for visitors, staff and students of Het Groene Woud
  • Walking Distance: This car park is an 8-minute walk from the HZ
  • Exit Procedure: Upon showing your entry card, you can get an exit card at the JRCZ service desk
Eco-Friendly Tip: Consider cycling or using public transport - HZ is well-connected and has excellent bike facilities! Vlissingen campus is within walking distance from the railway station.

HZ has a comprehensive accident reporting procedure to ensure safety and learn from incidents:

Definition: An (near-)accident is any incident where an employee, student, or visitor at an HZ location or during HZ activities has sustained or could have sustained injury, or where material damage has occurred.
Immediate Reporting Steps
  1. Report to supervisor/instructor: Immediately inform your supervisor, instructor, SLC, or teacher to ensure proper handling
  2. Fill out the digital form: Report the incident via the digital Accident Reporting Form on the service portal
  3. Timeline: Report as soon as possible, but within 5 working days maximum
For Students
  • Report the incident to your instructor/teacher/SLC immediately
  • Complete the digital Accident Reporting Form on the service portal
  • You can fill out the form together with the instructor/teacher if needed
For Employees
  • Report to your supervisor as soon as possible
  • If supervisor is unavailable, report to the next higher supervisor
  • Complete the digital Accident Reporting Form on the service portal
  • If a student reports an accident to you, help them fill out the form together
Serious Accidents: Accidents resulting in death, hospitalization, or permanent injury must be reported IMMEDIATELY to the Dutch Labour Inspectorate at 0800-5151 (24/7 available)
Purpose of Reporting
  • Safety improvement: Learn from incidents to prevent future accidents
  • Legal compliance: Meet workplace safety legislation requirements
  • Analysis and prevention: Enable organizational analysis and improvement measures
  • Support: Ensure proper care and follow-up for those involved
Privacy Note: Personal data is handled confidentially and only used for accident analysis and prevention purposes. External reporting may require personal information.

A passport-sized photo is mandatory for receiving your HZ-Card, which you need for parking access, printing, scanning, equipment borrowing, and HZ catering purchases.

Important: If your photo doesn't meet the requirements, your student card cannot be created during events like HZ Kick-off.
Technical Requirements
  • File format: .jpg, .jpeg, .gif, or .png
  • File size: 3MB or smaller
  • Quality: Clear and recent passport photo of passport quality
Photo Content Requirements
Required
  • Only you in the picture - no other people visible
  • Head and shoulders visible
  • Entire face and both eyes clearly visible
  • Hair clearly visible
  • Clear image with no dark spots or shadows
  • Head covering on religious grounds is permitted
Not Acceptable
  • Sunglasses or eyes not visible
  • Multiple faces in the photo
  • Poor quality/unrecognizable
  • Non-religious head covering
  • Digitally edited photos
  • Profile view (only part of face visible)
  • Too close, too far, blurry, or too light
Upload Process via MyHZ
  1. Go to MyHZ portal
  2. Enter your HZ username and password, then click Sign in
  3. Click on Change my profile
  4. Click on the text "Change photo" underneath the photo placeholder
  5. Follow the on-screen instructions to upload your photo
Common Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Part of head not visible in the photo
  • Photo taken from too close or too far away
  • Blurry or overexposed images
  • Profile shots showing only part of the face
Pro Tip: Take your photo in good lighting conditions, face the camera directly, and ensure your entire face is clearly visible. A plain background works best for passport-quality photos.

The Self Service Portal language can be customized through your Personal Profile:

Default Behavior: By default, the Self Service Portal is displayed in the language of your Internet browser.
Changing the Language
  1. Access your Personal Profile: Log into the Self Service Portal and navigate to your Personal Profile section
  2. Language Settings: Look for language or localization settings within your profile
  3. Select Preferred Language: Choose your preferred language from the available options
  4. Save Changes: Apply and save your language preference
Browser Language Override: If you want to use a different language than your browser's default, you must change it through your Personal Profile settings rather than relying on browser language detection.
Available Languages

The Self Service Portal typically supports multiple languages including:

  • Dutch (Nederlands)
  • English
  • Other languages may be available depending on HZ's current language support
Pro Tip: If you're having trouble finding the language settings, look for a gear/settings icon or "Profile" menu in the Self Service Portal interface. The language setting is usually found in personal preferences or account settings.
