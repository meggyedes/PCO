My Programming Journey: From Elementary School to HZ University

Over a decade ago, I took my first steps into the world of programming as a curious elementary school student. What began as weekend sessions learning Scratch has evolved into a comprehensive journey through the Hungarian education system and now to the Netherlands, where I'm pursuing modern ICT education at HZ University. This is the story of my programming evolution, the skills I've gained, and the passion that continues to drive me forward.

The Beginning: LogiSchool and Weekend Adventures

My programming journey began in elementary school when my siblings and I started attending LogiSchool on weekends. These weren't just casual computer classes – they were intensive 4-hour programming sessions that would shape my future in ways I couldn't have imagined at the time.

We started with Scratch, the visual programming language designed to introduce children to coding concepts. I remember the excitement of dragging and dropping colorful blocks to create animations and simple games. What seemed like play was actually building the foundation of logical thinking and problem-solving skills that would prove invaluable later.

After about 1-2 years of Scratch, we progressed to actual coding. The transition from visual blocks to text-based programming was challenging but thrilling. Suddenly, we were writing real code and creating Android applications that could be downloaded and used by others. The moment I saw my first app running on a phone was magical – I had created something tangible from lines of code.

Those weekend sessions at LogiSchool weren't just about learning syntax or memorizing commands. They were about discovering a passion. I found myself looking forward to weekends not for cartoons or games, but for the opportunity to solve puzzles, create something new, and push the boundaries of what I could build with code.

Secondary School: Building a Professional Foundation

When it came time to choose a secondary school, my path was clear. I enrolled in the ICT program at Bláthy Ottó Titusz Budapest, a decision that would transform my hobby into a professional skill set. Within this program, I specialized in Software Development and Testing, diving deep into the theoretical and practical aspects of software engineering.

The program was comprehensive and demanding. Unlike the exploratory nature of my elementary school programming, this was structured, professional education. We learned not just how to code, but how to code well – following best practices, understanding design patterns, and thinking about software architecture from the ground up.

Upon completing the program, I obtained a professional certification that validated my skills and knowledge. But more importantly, I had developed a diverse technical foundation that would serve me well in my continued studies and future career aspirations.

Technical Skills Acquired

During my time at Bláthy Ottó Titusz Budapest, I gained proficiency in a wide range of technologies and methodologies that form the backbone of modern software development:

Programming Languages
  • Python: Data analysis, automation, and backend development
  • JavaScript: Frontend interactivity and modern web development
  • C#: Object-oriented programming and .NET applications
  • PHP: Server-side scripting and web development
  • TypeScript: Type-safe JavaScript for larger applications
Web Technologies
  • HTML & CSS: Semantic markup and responsive design
  • React.js: Component-based frontend development
  • Node.js: JavaScript runtime for backend services
  • Vite: Modern build tool for faster development
  • Electron.js: Cross-platform desktop applications
  • Ajax: Asynchronous web communication
Frameworks & Tools
  • Laravel: PHP framework for elegant web applications
  • .NET MAUI: Cross-platform application development
  • Entity Framework: Object-relational mapping for .NET
Database Management
  • SQL: Structured query language for data manipulation
  • MariaDB: Open-source relational database system
Networking & Methodologies
  • Packet Tracker: Network simulation and design
  • Software Testing: Quality assurance methodologies
  • Agility Training: Modern development practices

This diverse technical foundation wasn't just about learning individual technologies – it was about understanding how they work together to create comprehensive solutions. I learned to think in terms of full-stack development, considering everything from database design to user interface, from network architecture to testing strategies.

The Fleet Management System: A Major Achievement

One of the most significant projects during my secondary education was the development of a comprehensive fleet management system. This wasn't just a school assignment – it was a collaborative team project designed to solve real-world problems for small to medium-sized companies.

The fleet management system we created was designed to help companies track their vehicles, manage maintenance schedules, monitor fuel consumption. Working as part of a development team, I experienced firsthand the challenges and rewards of collaborative software development.

The project was a resounding success, demonstrating not only our technical skills but also our ability to work together, manage deadlines, and deliver a product that met real business needs. The system we built was robust, user-friendly, and scalable – qualities that would make it viable in the commercial market.

In fact, the project was so successful that I have plans to develop it further into a market-ready product when time permits. The experience taught me that programming isn't just about writing code – it's about solving problems, creating value, and making a positive impact through technology.

The Transition: From Hungary to the Netherlands

After completing my secondary education in Hungary, I faced an important decision: where to continue my studies? While I had already gained substantial technical skills and professional certification, I knew that the rapidly evolving field of ICT required continuous learning and adaptation.

I chose to pursue my higher education in the Netherlands at HZ University of Applied Sciences for several compelling reasons. The Dutch education system's emphasis on practical, hands-on learning aligned perfectly with my learning style and career goals. The HBO-ICT program promised to build upon my existing foundation while introducing me to cutting-edge technologies and methodologies.

The international environment at HZ also appealed to me. In today's globalized tech industry, the ability to work with diverse teams and understand different perspectives is invaluable. Studying in the Netherlands provides me with this international experience while allowing me to improve my English and gain exposure to different approaches to problem-solving.

The transition hasn't been without challenges. Adapting to a new educational system, language, and culture requires effort and patience. However, my solid technical foundation from Hungary has given me confidence, and I'm excited to see how my existing skills will evolve and expand through the HBO-ICT program.

Looking Forward: Goals and Aspirations

As I continue my journey at HZ University, I'm excited about the opportunities that lie ahead. The courses I'm taking – from Programming Basics to Object-Oriented Programming, from Framework Projects to Personal Professional Development – are helping me refine my skills and discover new areas of interest.

My goal is to become a well-rounded ICT professional who can contribute meaningfully to the technology industry. Whether that's as a full-stack developer, a software architect, or perhaps even as an entrepreneur bringing innovative solutions to market, I'm keeping my options open while building a strong foundation.

I'm particularly interested in how emerging technologies like artificial intelligence, machine learning, and cloud computing are reshaping the industry. The skills I've gained so far provide a solid base for exploring these cutting-edge areas.

Conclusion: A Journey of Continuous Growth

Reflecting on my programming journey from those first Scratch programs in elementary school to my current studies at HZ University, I'm struck by how much I've learned and grown. But perhaps more importantly, I'm excited by how much more there is to discover.

The key lessons I've learned along the way are that programming is not just about technical skills – it's about creativity, problem-solving, persistence, and continuous learning. Every challenge I've faced, from debugging my first Android app to collaborating on complex team projects, has taught me something valuable.

As I continue my studies at HZ University and prepare for my future career in ICT, I carry with me the passion that was first ignited during those weekend sessions at LogiSchool. That curiosity and enthusiasm for technology continues to drive me forward, and I'm excited to see where this journey will take me next.

To fellow students who might be reading this: embrace every opportunity to learn, don't be afraid to take on challenging projects, and remember that every expert was once a beginner. The journey is just as important as the destination, and every line of code you write is a step forward in your own programming adventure.
